## Consonants (व्यञ्जनानि - Vyañjanāni)

### Organization

Sanskrit consonants are systematically organized by:
1. **Place of articulation** (5 vargas + additional sounds)
2. **Manner of articulation** (stops, nasals, approximants, sibilants)
3. **Voicing** (voiced vs. voiceless)
4. **Aspiration** (aspirated vs. unaspirated)

---

## Stop Consonants (Sparśāḥ - "Contacts")

### Five Vargas (25 stops total)

Each varga has 5 consonants organized systematically:
- **Row 1:** Voiceless unaspirated
- **Row 2:** Voiceless aspirated
- **Row 3:** Voiced unaspirated
- **Row 4:** Voiced aspirated
- **Row 5:** Nasal (voiced, uses nasal passage)

#### 1. Velar/Guttural (Ka-varga - कवर्ग)
**Place:** Velum (soft palate)

| Devanagari | IAST | IPA | Description |
|------------|------|-----|-------------|
| क | ka | /k/ | Voiceless velar stop |
| ख | kha | /kʰ/ | Voiceless aspirated velar stop |
| ग | ga | /g/ | Voiced velar stop |
| घ | gha | /gʰ/ | Voiced aspirated velar stop |
| ङ | ṅa | /ŋ/ | Velar nasal |

**English approximations:**
- क /k/ - "k" in "skill" (no aspiration)
- ख /kʰ/ - "k" in "kill" (with strong breath)
- ग /g/ - "g" in "go"
- घ /gʰ/ - "g" + "h" as in "doghouse"
- ङ /ŋ/ - "ng" in "sing"

#### 2. Palatal (Ca-varga - चवर्ग)
**Place:** Hard palate (roof of mouth behind alveolar ridge)

| Devanagari | IAST | IPA | Description |
|------------|------|-----|-------------|
| च | ca | /c/ or /tɕ/ | Voiceless palatal stop/affricate |
| छ | cha | /cʰ/ or /tɕʰ/ | Voiceless aspirated palatal stop/affricate |
| ज | ja | /ɟ/ or /dʑ/ | Voiced palatal stop/affricate |
| झ | jha | /ɟʰ/ or /dʑʰ/ | Voiced aspirated palatal stop/affricate |
| ञ | ña | /ɲ/ | Palatal nasal |

**English approximations:**
- च /c/ - "ch" in "church" (but without lip rounding)
- छ /cʰ/ - "ch" in "church" + strong breath
- ज /ɟ/ - "j" in "judge" (but without lip rounding)
- झ /ɟʰ/ - "j" + "h" as in "hedgehog"
- ञ /ɲ/ - "ny" in "canyon" or Spanish "ñ"

**Note:** Classical pronunciation may have been true palatal stops [c ɟ] rather than affricates [tɕ dʑ], but both reconstructions exist.

#### 3. Retroflex/Cerebral (Ṭa-varga - टवर्ग)
**Place:** Retroflex (tongue curled back to touch hard palate)

| Devanagari | IAST | IPA | Description |
|------------|------|-----|-------------|
| ट | ṭa | /ʈ/ | Voiceless retroflex stop |
| ठ | ṭha | /ʈʰ/ | Voiceless aspirated retroflex stop |
| ड | ḍa | /ɖ/ | Voiced retroflex stop |
| ढ | ḍha | /ɖʰ/ | Voiced aspirated retroflex stop |
| ण | ṇa | /ɳ/ | Retroflex nasal |

**English approximations:**
- ट /ʈ/ - "t" with tongue curled back (NO English equivalent)
- ठ /ʈʰ/ - aspirated ट
- ड /ɖ/ - "d" with tongue curled back
- ढ /ɖʰ/ - aspirated ड
- ण /ɳ/ - "n" with tongue curled back

**Critical:** These are TRUE RETROFLEXES. The tongue tip curls back and contacts the hard palate/alveolar ridge from behind. This is DISTINCT from dentals and is a key feature of Sanskrit phonology.

#### 4. Dental (Ta-varga - तवर्ग)
**Place:** Teeth (tongue touches back of upper front teeth)

| Devanagari | IAST | IPA | Description |
|------------|------|-----|-------------|
| त | ta | /t̪/ | Voiceless dental stop |
| थ | tha | /t̪ʰ/ | Voiceless aspirated dental stop |
| द | da | /d̪/ | Voiced dental stop |
| ध | dha | /d̪ʰ/ | Voiced aspirated dental stop |
| न | na | /n̪/ | Dental nasal |

**English approximations:**
- त /t̪/ - "t" in "stop" BUT with tongue at teeth (like Spanish/French "t")
- थ /t̪ʰ/ - aspirated त
- द /d̪/ - "d" BUT with tongue at teeth (like Spanish/French "d")
- ध /d̪ʰ/ - aspirated द
- न /n̪/ - "n" with tongue at teeth

**Critical:** English "t" and "d" are ALVEOLAR (tongue at alveolar ridge), but Sanskrit dental sounds have the tongue touching the TEETH. This contrasts with retroflex.

#### 5. Labial (Pa-varga - पवर्ग)
**Place:** Lips (bilabial)

| Devanagari | IAST | IPA | Description |
|------------|------|-----|-------------|
| प | pa | /p/ | Voiceless bilabial stop |
| फ | pha | /pʰ/ | Voiceless aspirated bilabial stop |
| ब | ba | /b/ | Voiced bilabial stop |
| भ | bha | /bʰ/ | Voiced aspirated bilabial stop |
| म | ma | /m/ | Bilabial nasal |

**English approximations:**
- प /p/ - "p" in "spin" (no aspiration)
- फ /pʰ/ - "p" in "pin" (with strong breath) - **NOT "f"**
- ब /b/ - "b" in "bin"
- भ /bʰ/ - "b" + "h" as in "clubhouse"
- म /m/ - "m" in "mom"

## Approximants (Antaḥsthāḥ - "Between sounds")

| Devanagari | IAST | IPA | Place | Description |
|------------|------|-----|-------|-------------|
| य | ya | /j/ | Palatal | Voiced palatal approximant |
| र | ra | /r/ | Alveolar/Retroflex | Voiced (retroflex) trill or tap |
| ल | la | /l/ | Dental | Voiced dental lateral approximant |
| व | va | /ʋ/ or /w/ | Labio-dental/Labio-velar | Voiced labio-dental approximant |

**English approximations:**
- य /j/ - "y" in "yes"
- र /r/ - Spanish/Italian rolled "r" OR retroflex "r" (debate exists)
- ल /l/ - "l" in "love" (dental position)
- व /ʋ/ - Between "v" and "w" (lips don't fully touch)

**Note on र:** Some scholars reconstruct this as a retroflex flap [ɽ], others as an alveolar trill [r]. Ancient texts describe it as involving tongue vibration.

## Sibilants (Ūṣmāṇaḥ - "Heated sounds")

| Devanagari | IAST | IPA | Place | Description |
|------------|------|-----|-------|-------------|
| श | śa | /ɕ/ | Palatal | Voiceless palatal sibilant |
| ष | ṣa | /ʂ/ | Retroflex | Voiceless retroflex sibilant |
| स | sa | /s/ | Dental/Alveolar | Voiceless alveolar sibilant |

**English approximations:**
- श /ɕ/ - "sh" in "sheep" BUT more fronted (like German "ich")
- ष /ʂ/ - "sh" with tongue curled back (retroflex "sh")
- स /s/ - "s" in "see"

**Critical:** In modern Indian traditions, श and ष have MERGED to /ʃ/. In classical Sanskrit, they were DISTINCT:
- श = palatal (tongue at hard palate)
- ष = retroflex (tongue curled back)
- स = alveolar (tongue at alveolar ridge)

This three-way distinction is essential for understanding sandhi rules and consonant classification.

## Special Symbols (Not phonemes, but important)

### Anusvāra (ं)
**Symbol:** ं (dot above)
**IAST:** ṃ
**Pronunciation:** Nasalization or homorganic nasal

**Function:** Represents nasal sound that assimilates to following consonant:
- Before क, ख, ग, घ → /ŋ/ (as in ङ)
- Before च, छ, ज, झ → /ɲ/ (as in ञ)
- Before ट, ठ, ड, ढ → /ɳ/ (as in ण)
- Before त, थ, द, ध → /n̪/ (as in न)
- Before प, फ, ब, भ → /m/ (as in म)
- Before य, र, ल, व, श, ष, स, ह → pure nasalization of preceding vowel

### Visarga (ः)
**Symbol:** ः (two dots)
**IAST:** ḥ
**IPA:** /h/ or /x/
**Pronunciation:** Voiceless glottal or velar fricative

**Function:** Represents:
1. Historical /s/ or /r/ in word-final position
2. Often colored by following sound in sandhi

### Avagraha (ऽ)
**Symbol:** ऽ
**Function:** Marks elision (omission) of initial अ after sandhi

## Distinctive Features

### Aspiration
Sanskrit distinguishes aspirated (महाप्राण mahāprāṇa) from unaspirated (अल्पप्राण alpaprāṇa) consonants:
- Aspirated: ख, घ, छ, झ, ठ, ढ, थ, ध, फ, भ
- Unaspirated: क, ग, च, ज, ट, ड, त, द, प, ब

Aspiration means a STRONG puff of air follows the consonant. This is PHONEMIC (changes meaning).

### Voicing
- Voiced (घोष ghoṣa): ग, घ, ज, झ, ड, ढ, द, ध, ब, भ, य, र, ल, व, ह (+ all nasals)
- Voiceless (अघोष aghoṣa): क, ख, च, छ, ट, ठ, त, थ, प, फ, श, ष, स

### Retroflexion
A key feature of Sanskrit (and Dravidian influence on Indo-Aryan):
- Retroflex consonants: ट, ठ, ड, ढ, ण, ष (and possibly र)
- Contrast with: Dental (त, थ, द, ध, न, स) and Alveolar

### Palatalization
Sanskrit has distinct palatal series:
- Palatal stops: च, छ, ज, झ
- Palatal nasal: ञ
- Palatal sibilant: श
- Palatal approximant: य

## Articulatory Classification (Traditional)

Sanskrit phonetics uses precise articulatory terminology:

### Places of Articulation (Sthāna)
1. **Kaṇṭha** (throat/velum) - Velars: क, ख, ग, घ, ङ, ह + vowels अ, आ
2. **Tālu** (palate) - Palatals: च, छ, ज, झ, ञ, य, श + vowels इ, ई
3. **Mūrdhan** (head/cerebral) - Retroflexes: ट, ठ, ड, ढ, ण, र, ष + vowels ऋ, ॠ
4. **Danta** (teeth) - Dentals: त, थ, द, ध, न, ल, स + vowels ऌ, ॡ
5. **Oṣṭha** (lips) - Labials: प, फ, ब, भ, म + vowels उ, ऊ
6. **Nāsikā** (nose) - Nasals: ङ, ञ, ण, न, म + anusvāra
7. **Kaṇṭha-tālu** (throat-palate) - Vowels: ए, ऐ
8. **Kaṇṭha-oṣṭha** (throat-lips) - Vowels: ओ, औ
9. **Danta-oṣṭha** (teeth-lips) - व

### Manners of Articulation (Prayatna)
1. **Sparśa** (contact) - Stops
2. **Īṣat-sparśa** (slight contact) - Approximants
3. **Ūṣman** (heated) - Sibilants and ह
4. **Nāsikya** (nasal) - Nasals

## Notes for Audio Recording

When recording classical Sanskrit pronunciation:

1. **Aspiration:** Make aspirated consonants CLEARLY distinct with strong breath
2. **Retroflexion:** Curl tongue back for ट, ठ, ड, ढ, ण, ष
3. **Dental position:** Touch teeth (not alveolar ridge) for त, थ, द, ध, न
4. **Sibilant distinction:** Maintain श /ɕ/ vs ष /ʂ/ vs स /s/ difference
5. **Vowel purity:** No schwa deletion—pronounce all vowels fully
6. **Short 'a':** Use /ɐ/ or /ʌ/ (open-mid), NOT schwa /ə/
7. **Flat intonation:** Classical Sanskrit has monotone reading, not pitch accent
